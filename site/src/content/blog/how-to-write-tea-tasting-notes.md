Good tasting notes aren't about being "correct". They're about building memory.
If you can write **three honest sentences** after a session, you're already doing it right.

## The 5‑line tea tasting notes template

1. **Context**: what tea, what day, what mood?
2. **Aroma**: what do you smell from dry leaf / wet leaf / cup?
3. **Taste**: 2-4 words (sweet, bitter, roasted, floral, mineral…).
4. **Texture**: thin/thick, drying/juicy, clean/oily.
5. **Next time**: one change you'll try (hotter water, shorter steep, more leaf).

## What matters most: comparisons

The fastest way to improve your palate is to compare:

- this tea vs last week's tea, or
- this infusion vs the previous infusion.

Write what changed.

## Common mistakes

- **Too many descriptors**: keep it short so you'll actually do it again.
- **No brewing parameters**: without temp/time/leaf amount, you can't learn.
- **Trying to sound fancy**: your notes are for you, not for a judge.
