Why keeping a tea journal changes how you taste tea

A tea journal isn’t about perfect descriptors — it’s about awareness. When you write a few lines after each session, you stop guessing and start remembering.

1) You build tasting memory

Your brain is great at feelings and terrible at timestamps. Notes create a trail: what you brewed, how it tasted, and what you changed.

2) You learn faster (because you can compare)

The biggest improvements come from comparisons: infusion 2 vs infusion 3, winter storage vs summer storage, 90°C vs 95°C. Without notes, it all blurs.

3) You find your preferences

Over time you’ll see patterns: what vendors you love, what roast levels work for you, and which teas shine with your water.
